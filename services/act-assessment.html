Diagnostic

The Agile Capability Test

A structured diagnostic across 12 dimensions. It tells you where you are strong, where you are fragile, and what to fix first. No sales pitch embedded. Just an accurate read.

Why start with a diagnostic?

Transformation programmes that skip the diagnostic tend to apply generic solutions to specific problems. Or they surface problems too late, after budget is spent and credibility is lost.

The ACT gives your leadership team an honest, evidence-based picture of where the organisation actually is - not where it thinks it is or where it says it wants to be. That picture shapes everything that comes after.

The assessment is administered through the MyKojo platform. Respondents are drawn from across leadership levels and team roles. Results are aggregated, benchmarked, and presented in a facilitated readout session with your leadership team.

Who completes it

Leadership team, product owners, scrum masters, team members. Cross-level perspective is essential - the diagnostic is designed to surface gaps between what leadership believes and what teams experience.

Time to complete

25-35 minutes per respondent. Administered online through MyKojo. Results available within 48 hours of final respondent submission.

What you receive

A full diagnostic report, dimension-by-dimension scoring, comparison against sector benchmarks, and a prioritised set of recommendations. Presented in a 2-hour leadership readout session.

The 12 dimensions

What the ACT measures.

01
Leadership Alignment
Do senior leaders share a coherent and accurate picture of what the transformation is for?
02
Team Structure
Are teams sized, scoped, and staffed to own and deliver value end to end?
03
Product Thinking
Is there genuine product ownership - empowered to set direction and make trade-offs?
04
Technical Practices
Are engineering practices keeping pace with delivery ambition?
05
Feedback Loops
How quickly does real information about outcomes reach the people who can act on it?
06
Governance & Funding
Does how you plan and fund work support or contradict how you say you want to work?
07
Culture & Safety
Can teams surface problems early without risk to themselves?
08
Practitioner Capability
Do the people running agile processes actually understand them - and can they coach others?
09
Customer Proximity
How close are delivery teams to the people they are building for? How often do they talk?
10
Dependency Management
How effectively does the organisation manage dependencies between teams and value streams?
11
Metrics & Measurement
Does the organisation measure what matters - outcomes, not just output and velocity?
12
Continuous Improvement
Is learning from failure a genuine organisational practice, or a retrospective box-ticking exercise?
