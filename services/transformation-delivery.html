Service

Transformation Delivery

Embedded practitioners who work alongside your teams to deliver the transformation - not consult on it from the outside. We take accountability for outcomes, not recommendations.

Skin in the game.

Most consultants write the report and leave. We embed. Our practitioners work inside your organisation, alongside your people, for the duration of the engagement. They are accountable for outcomes - not for the quality of the slide deck.

Every Transformation Delivery engagement is scoped from the ACT assessment. We know where your gaps are before we start, which means we do not waste your first three months trying to understand the problem. We start working on it.

Delivery is structured in 90-day cycles. Each cycle has defined outcomes that your leadership team agrees to before the cycle starts. At the end of each cycle, you see measurable evidence of improvement - or we have a direct conversation about why not.

Engagement model

Minimum three-month engagement. Typically two to four practitioners embedded full or part time, depending on organisation size and scope. Led by a senior practitioner who attends your leadership reviews.

Accountability

Each 90-day cycle has agreed outcomes defined in measurable terms before it starts. Cycle review is conducted with your leadership team. We do not gate progress reviews behind additional fees.

How it works

90 days at a time.

Day 1-14

ACT Baseline

If not already completed, we run the ACT assessment across the relevant teams and leadership levels. This establishes the baseline and scopes the first 90-day cycle.

Day 15-90

Cycle One

Embedded delivery against agreed outcomes. Practitioners work inside your teams. Weekly check-in with your sponsor. Blockers surfaced and resolved in real time - not in the next quarterly review.

Day 90

Cycle Review

Measured outcomes reviewed against the ACT baseline. Where we hit the mark, we show it. Where we did not, we explain why and agree what changes in the next cycle. Your decision whether to continue.
